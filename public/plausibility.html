Key Findings

Dominant violation: ETA/ARR (S07) timestamps occurring AFTER Delivery Estimated (POD_EST/S31) — accounting for ~75% of all violations. This suggests POD_EST dates are often set too early relative to actual vessel arrival times.
SC4 vs SC3: SC4 started at 72% violation rate (CW01) and improved dramatically to 12% by CW07. SC3 remains stable at 5-8%.
BCO shipments consistently show the highest violation rates across weeks (13-38%), indicating more complex routing challenges.

Methodology

What we check: The expected milestone sequence is:
S02 (Collected) → S04 (Departed) → S07 (Arrived) → S31 (Delivered)

KPI Definition: % of shipments where at least one milestone timestamp violates the expected chronological order.

Data Sources: SC3 (NGTM system) provides Collected, ETD, ATD, ETA, ATA, Delivered timestamps. SC4 adds planned/estimated dates (TARGET_COLLECTION, DELIVERY_DATE_ACT_EST_PLAN).

Note: Placeholder dates (year ≤ 1901) are excluded. Each milestone pair is checked only when both timestamps are available.
